This page explains what Rule-Based Biology is, why it exists, and how to explore this site.

What This Site Is

Rule-Based Biology is a reconstruction of biological science based on the discovery of governing rules that determine how biological systems operate.

It is not a theory, not a reinterpretation, and not an extension of existing biology. It is a framework built from identifying constraints that must hold across all biological systems.

Why This Site Exists

Modern biology has produced enormous volumes of data, yet has failed to converge on a coherent understanding of life.

This is not due to lack of effort or experimentation, but due to the absence of governing rules capable of organizing that data into a consistent framework.

This site exists to present what emerges once those rules are identified.

The Central Idea

Rules give data meaning.

Without rules, observations remain isolated. With rules, biology becomes predictable, coherent, and mechanistic.

This site documents that transition.

How to Use This Site

The material is organized as a logical progression:

  • Why Biology Failed — explains the structural problem
  • Why Rules Matter — explains why rules are necessary
  • Rule-Based Biology — presents the new framework
  • Applications — shows what becomes possible
  • AI & the Future — explains broader implications
  • Students & Careers — explains what this means for learners

The pages are designed to be read in order, though each can stand on its own.

What This Is Not

  • Not a speculative theory
  • Not a philosophical argument
  • Not an alternative interpretation of biology
  • Not dependent on belief or consensus

This work is grounded in consistency, constraint, and logical necessity.

Biology becomes intelligible when rules are known.
This site documents that realization.
